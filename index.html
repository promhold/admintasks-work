The best thing you will ever receive that is free

Right now, in the middle of your workday, there is something more valuable than any salary, tax refund, or pay rise. It has already been paid for. It cannot be earned, calculated, or negotiated. It is simply received.

Here is the truth. Every person carries the weight of falling short of God and who we were made to be. That weight has a cost. But 2,000 years ago, Jesus Christ entered the world, lived a sinless life, and chose to carry that cost Himself. He died on the cross in our place. Three days later He rose from the dead, and in doing so, He proved that death itself had no power over Him.

"For this is how God loved the world: He gave his one and only Son, so that everyone who believes in him will not perish but have eternal life." John 3:16 NLT

Eternal life is not a reward for good behaviour. It is a gift. You receive it the same way you receive any gift. Simply accept it. That moment of accepting Jesus is the beginning of a relationship. Not religion. A genuine, living, personal relationship with the God who made you and loves you completely.

"If you openly declare that Jesus is Lord and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved." Romans 10:9 NLT

Not a long checklist. Not years of trying harder. One decision. One moment. A life changed forever.

A prayer from your heart

This is not a ritual or a formula. Saying these words alone will not save you. What matters is your heart. Read each line slowly. Pause. Mean it. Speak to God as honestly as you can, the way you would speak to someone who is truly listening, because He is.

Lord Jesus,

I come to You just as I am. I know I have fallen short and I cannot fix that on my own.

I believe You were born into this world, that You lived without sin, and that You died on the cross to pay for everything I have done wrong.

I believe You rose from the dead on the third day, alive and victorious over death.

I accept Your forgiveness as a gift I could never earn. From this moment, You are my Lord. I am Yours.

Thank You for loving me.

Amen.

If you prayed that with a sincere heart, welcome. Heaven is rejoicing over you right now.

"In the same way, there is joy in the presence of God's angels when even one sinner repents." Luke 15:10 NLT

You are not the same person you were a moment ago. Here are a few steps to take from here:

  • Tell someone you trust what you just decided.
  • Find a Bible-believing church and get connected to a community of faith.
  • Begin reading the Bible. The New King James Version (NKJV) or the New Living Translation (NLT) are both excellent for clear, faithful reading.
  • Talk to God every day. Prayer is simply an honest conversation.

This is the beginning of something real.
