Frequently asked questions

What is overtime pay?

Overtime pay is the additional compensation required by law when an employee works more than a set number of hours in a workweek. Under the federal Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), most employees must be paid at least 1.5 times their regular rate of pay for every hour worked beyond 40 hours in a single workweek.

Who qualifies for overtime?

Most hourly workers are entitled to overtime. Salaried employees earning less than $684/week ($35,568/year) are also generally covered. However, certain "exempt" employees — including executives, administrators, professionals, and some computer workers — may not qualify. Always check with your HR department or an employment attorney if you're unsure.

How is overtime calculated for salaried employees?

For non-exempt salaried employees, your regular rate of pay is calculated by dividing your weekly salary by the number of hours you're expected to work. If your annual salary is $45,000, your weekly rate is $45,000 ÷ 52 = $865.38, and your hourly rate is $865.38 ÷ 40 = $21.63. Overtime hours are then paid at 1.5× that rate.

Does my employer have to pay overtime?

If you're a non-exempt employee under the FLSA, your employer is legally required to pay overtime for hours worked beyond 40 in a workweek. Some states have additional requirements. Employers cannot require employees to waive their right to overtime pay, and retaliation against employees for asserting overtime rights is illegal.

What is "time and a half"?

Time and a half means your regular hourly rate multiplied by 1.5. For example, if you earn $20/hr normally, your overtime rate is $30/hr. This is the federal minimum for overtime — your employer may pay more, but not less.

Is overtime calculated daily or weekly?

Under federal law, overtime is calculated weekly (over 40 hours per week). Some states — like California — also require daily overtime for hours over 8 in a single workday, whichever results in more overtime pay. Use the state selector above to get the right calculation for your situation.
